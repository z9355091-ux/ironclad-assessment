### SAMREF — What Is It?
- **SAMREF** = Saudi Aramco Mobil Refinery Company — joint venture between Saudi Aramco and ExxonMobil
- Located in **Yanbu Industrial City**, on Saudi Arabia's Red Sea coast (west coast — NOT the Gulf side)
- Capacity: ~400,000 bpd
- Yanbu is a major Saudi export hub; oil piped from Eastern Province via the **Petroline (East-West Pipeline)** — the same pipeline cited as the only meaningful alternative to Hormuz
- **Critical significance:** Yanbu is the western terminus of the Petroline. If Yanbu refining infrastructure is struck, the Petroline alternative route is compromised at its endpoint

### Analytical Significance — The Petroline Is Now Threatened
The Hormuz alternative routing analysis (Petroline 4M bpd + Habshan-Fujairah 2M bpd = ~8M bpd replacement) assumed those pipelines' western endpoints were safe. A drone strike on SAMREF Yanbu means:
- The **alternative route's exit point is now under attack**
- Even if oil flows through the Petroline, if Yanbu refining/export capacity is disrupted, the bypass is degraded
- The "8M bpd alternative" figure may be further reduced
- The conflict has expanded geographically from the Gulf to the Red Sea corridor
