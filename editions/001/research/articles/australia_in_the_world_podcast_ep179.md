## Summary (from episode description)

Two weeks into the US-Israeli war on Iran. Darren Lim applies **Robert Pape's cost-benefit framework** (Bombing to Win, 1996) to assess where the war stands.

---

## Key Claims

### Tactical vs. Strategic Divergence
- **Tactical achievements are real:** Two-thirds of Iran's missile launchers destroyed; Iran's navy sunk; Iranian leadership decapitated
- **BUT:** Probability of converting tactical gains into durable strategic outcomes is **low**
- Cost-benefit framework: the costs are mounting faster than the strategic benefits are materialising

### Military sustainability problem
- **Interceptor stocks** being depleted at unsustainable rates (Semafor, March 15: Israel running "critically low on interceptors")
- **Missile defence assets may be redeployed from South Korea** — "a move that achieves what Chinese coercion could not"
  - This is the most significant strategic second-order effect: weakening South Korea's air defence to sustain the Iran campaign
  - China and North Korea are the beneficiaries

### Economic cascade
- Iran's effective closure of Hormuz has triggered "what may be the **largest oil supply disruption in history**"
- Cascading effects through: gas → fertiliser → food markets
- "Arriving at the worst possible moment in the agricultural calendar"

### Strategic landscape
- Russia is profiting
- China is **learning** (observing how Western air defence works, what interceptor depletion looks like)
- Gulf allies are **furious** (Qatar struck, UAE threatened, US knew nothing of South Pars strike)
- **Non-proliferation incentive structure has been inverted**: Iran is being attacked precisely because it did NOT have nuclear weapons; every non-nuclear state now reconsiders

### Outcome range
- **Optimistic:** Painful but temporary shock; Iran sues for terms; Hormuz reopens in months
- **Pessimistic:** Nuclear-armed Iran within **18 months**
  - Iran's leadership has been decapitated; no diplomatic off-ramp; hardliners control succession
  - Iran's stated determination: "this is the last time it is attacked" — nuclear deterrence as the logical conclusion

### US-Israel divergence
- Trump's signals are contradictory (simultaneously claiming victory and distancing from South Pars strike)
- US-Israel coordination has broken down on key decisions (South Pars struck without US knowledge)
- De-escalation path requires US to manage an ally it cannot fully control

### The "Weaponised Interdependence" conclusion
> "A sanctioned middle power with cheap drones and a narrow strait has exposed the gap between America's capacity to destroy and its capacity to control."
