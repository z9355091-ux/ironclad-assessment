# Lowy Institute — "Securing Australia's fuel supplies in a major conflict"

**Source:** Lowy Institute, The Interpreter  
**URL:** https://www.lowyinstitute.org/the-interpreter/securing-australia-s-fuel-supplies-major-conflict  
**Date:** 2025-2026 (pre-war analysis, more relevant than ever)  
**Reliability:** HIGH — detailed quantitative analysis with government source citations

---

## Core Argument

The 90-day IEA target is insufficient for a prolonged conflict. Even if met, it would not sustain Australia through a major supply disruption lasting 6-12 months. Gas-to-liquids (GTL) technology is an available, enduring, and fundable solution.

---

## Key Quantitative Data

### Australia's consumption and production
- **Peacetime consumption:** ~1 million bpd (confirmed by government report, 2020)
- **Critical services requirement (defence, power generation, etc):** ~26% of 1M bpd = ~260,000 bpd
- **Two remaining refineries:** Ampol Lytton + Viva Energy Geelong = **230,000 bpd combined** (23% of 1M bpd needed)
- **Domestic crude oil reserves:** Running out by **2030** (Geoscience Australia)
- **Condensate reserves:** Last until ~**2039** — but condensate is much less suited for fuel refinement; post-2030 domestic production might meet only **10% of needs**

### The IEA compliance gap
- IEA standard: 90 days of stocks
- Australia's actual holding (September 2025): **52 days** — still non-compliant
- Australia is "the only IEA member that for years hasn't met the standard of 90 days"
- Note: Leaney transcript cited 30-32 days; the 52 days (Sep 2025) may include contractual offshore holdings

### What fuel powers
- **98% of Australia's transport sector**, including food distribution
- **83% of coal mining operations** (which sustain much of the energy grid)
- **Essentially all ADF operations**

## The Gas-to-Liquids (GTL) Solution

### Reference facility: Shell Pearl GTL (Qatar)
- Converts 1.6 billion cubic feet/day of natural gas → **140,000 bpd of fuel + 120,000 bpd other liquids**
- Cost (today): ~**$33 billion**
- Build time: ~**4 years**

### Applying GTL to Australia
| Plants | Capacity | Cost | Online by | % of 1M bpd |
|--------|----------|------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | 140,000 bpd | $33B | 2031 (if started 2027) | 14% |
| 3 | 420,000 bpd | $99B | By 2039 | 42% |

- Feedstock: **Coal seam gas** — won't deplete until ~2060
- No new oil or gas exploration required (politically viable)
- In wartime: gas exports would be cut off anyway → GTL uses stranded gas for domestic fuel
- GTL can also produce **chemical precursors** — addresses Balaji's chemical precursor shortage concern

### Funding sources identified
- **AUKUS nuclear-powered submarine program**: $368B total — "a ripe plum"
- Rising defence budget
- Allied investment (allies would benefit in wartime)
- **Australian superannuation funds**: need to invest $3.2B/week — infrastructure-scale investment

---

## The Naval Convoy Logic

- 6,100 cargo vessels serve Australia's import/export needs (650 fuel and chemical tankers)
- Too many to protect in wartime
- Without bulk carriers, livestock carriers, vehicle carriers: ~600 critical cargo ships remain
- Possibly as few as **50 ships** (per Strategic Fleet Report) could meet critical needs — feasible to protect with Navy
- GTL enables this: if domestic fuel production meets critical needs, Australia can reduce import dependency to a convoy-scale operation

## Analytical Use for Assessment

- **Quantitative backbone** for Australia's vulnerability argument (1M bpd consumption, 23% domestic, 52-day stocks)
- **2030 cliff** — domestic crude runs out; within 4 years Australia's refining capacity becomes entirely import-dependent for feedstock
- **GTL as the policy recommendation** — specific, costed, fundable; far more concrete than "expand reserves"
- **SSN funding redirect argument** — politically provocative but analytically valid; $368B submarine program vs. $99B for fuel independence
- **The ADF dependency** — military operations run on liquid fuel; Australia cannot fight a war without imported fuel under current settings
