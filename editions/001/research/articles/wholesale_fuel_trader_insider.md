## Key Claims

### 1. Australia's Import Dependency & Reserves
- Australian fuel is **90% imported**, mainly from Asia
- Australia has **not met the IEA 90-day buffer for over a decade** — sits at roughly **32 days of stock**
- Fault of both Labor and Liberal governments
- Easy to store crude oil; **very difficult and expensive to store refined products** (flammable, degrade after months) — this is why commercial actors don't build new storage; explains why the market won't solve the reserve problem

### 2. Crude Oil Mismatch Problem (Critical — not in transcripts)
- Asian refineries are configured for **medium sour crude** (primarily Middle Eastern origin)
- US strategic reserve releases are **light sweet crude** — **incompatible with Asian refinery configurations**
- Therefore: US SPR releases do NOT help Australia/Asia — the crude types don't match the refinery hardware
- "It doesn't matter how many barrels the US releases" — it can't replace lost Middle Eastern supply into Asian refineries
- **This is the key structural reason why Australian fuel cannot easily be re-sourced**

### 3. Export Bans Compounding the Crisis
- Asian refiners are **cancelling contracts**
- Thailand and China are **banning diesel and petrol exports** to protect domestic supply
- Result: Very expensive to source alternative cargoes for Australia
- **MOPS (Mean of Platts Singapore) Premium has skyrocketed** — the benchmark for Australian fuel pricing
- **Backwardation** has spiked (futures cheaper than spot — market pricing in near-term scarcity)

### 4. Refined Product vs Crude Price Divergence (Important for pricing argument)
- Brent crude has risen ~40% ($70 → $100/bbl)
- But **refined products (diesel, petrol, jet fuel) have spiked far higher** than crude — regional supply/demand
- Australian fuel priced as: **Singapore fuel + ocean freight + local costs** — NOT indexed to Brent
- Diesel facing worse constraints than petrol; **jet fuel worst of all**
- Critical for Byrne's rockets/feathers argument: the pump price spike exceeds crude price movement

### 5. Regional Australia Wholesale Diesel Crisis
- Oil majors (Mobil, BP, Ampol) **stopped selling on the wholesale market** — holding product for own retail stations
- Independent wholesalers (who supply regional Australia, farms) **buy on the spot wholesale market only** — no term supply guarantees
- Oil majors **exited regional Australia delivery years ago** — independent wholesalers filled that gap
- Now those independents can't get supply → **regional Australia fuel crisis** worse than cities
- Mining industry in Australia runs on diesel → existential risk to supply chains

### 6. Refinery Operating Constraints
- Cannot simply shut down a refinery — designed to run 24/7
- Complete shutdown risks **serious equipment damage**
- Refiners choosing to run at **~50% capacity** to conserve crude feedstock without shutting down entirely

### 7. Normalisation Timeline
- Corroborates Leaney: **1.5 to 2 months** after Hormuz opens before Asian refiners return to full capacity
- Author's assessment: "Australia — and most countries — do not have stored fuel that will last this long even with rationing"

### 8. Price Dynamics
- Commodity trading principle: "High prices cure high prices, low prices cure low prices"
- Supply cannot increase right now — only lever is **demand destruction via high prices**
- If governments cap prices at low levels: demand stays high, supply keeps falling, Australia runs out entirely
- **Running out of fuel is worse than having some fuel at a high price**
- Author implies rationing should already be in place
