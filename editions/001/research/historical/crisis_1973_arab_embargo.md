# 1973 Arab Oil Embargo (OPEC)

## Crisis Overview

**Trigger/Cause:** October Yom Kippur War (Egypt & Syria attacking Israeli-occupied territories on Oct 6, 1973) → OAPEC (Organization of Arab Petroleum Exporting Countries) embargo in response to US military support for Israel

**Duration:** October 1973 – March 1974 (6 months)

**Key Players:**
- Saudi Arabia (led by King Faisal): Orchestrated the embargo
- OAPEC members: Algeria, Iraq, Libya, Kuwait, Qatar, Abu Dhabi, Saudi Arabia
- US President Richard Nixon provided $2.2B in emergency arms to Israel (Oct 19), triggering the embargo (Oct 20)

## Price Impact

- **Pre-crisis:** $3.00/barrel (Sept 1973)
- **Peak:** $11.65/barrel (Dec 1973, Vienna OPEC conference)
- **Percentage increase:** ~290-300% increase over 6 months
- **Global economic impact:** 400% spike globally by end-crisis; created stagflation, unemployment, global recession
- **US economy:** Federal Trade Administration estimated embargo caused 500,000 American job losses and $10-20B GNP loss; transformed Western economic policy toward energy independence

## Global Economic Impact

- **GDP contraction:** Steepest economic contraction since Great Depression
- **Inflation:** Sharp inflationary spiral globally, particularly severe in Western nations
- **Employment:** Widespread unemployment due to recession
- **Demand destruction:** American vehicle market shifted dramatically from "gas guzzlers" to fuel-efficient Japanese cars; US auto industry declined
- **Consumer behavior:** Gasoline rationing, long lines at pump stations, speed limits introduced
- **Energy policy:** Led to Western pivot toward alternative energy research, nuclear power, coal switching, strategic reserves establishment

## Australian Impact

The research materials focus on the 1973 embargo's impact on global economy, but specific Australian refinery status at the time not yet detailed. Australia was at the end of a long supply chain and imported significant oil, making it vulnerable to price shocks.

## Policy Responses (Global)

- **US:** Created Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR), implemented Project Independence (energy independence plan), Emergency Petroleum Allocation Act (Nov 1973)
- **OAPEC:** Gradual monthly production cuts (25% by December from September levels)
- **Western nations:** Initiated massive energy alternative research programs, nuclear power expansion
- **Diplomatic:** Kissinger led negotiations; embargo lifted March 18, 1974 after US promised "evenhanded" Middle East policy and weapon sales to Saudi Arabia

## Key Insights

- Embargo **failed** to achieve stated political objectives (Israel withdrawal) but succeeded in economic disruption
- Saudi Arabia's 4% share of US oil was insufficient to cause severe disruption alone, but multiplier effect through Arab politics created global price escalation
- This crisis marked the end of post-WWII "long summer" of prosperity (1945-1973) in the West
- Triggered long-term energy policy reforms in Western nations
