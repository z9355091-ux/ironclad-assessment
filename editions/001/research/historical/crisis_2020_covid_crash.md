# 2020 COVID Oil Crash & OPEC+ Response

## Crisis Overview

**Trigger/Cause:** 
1. COVID-19 pandemic (demand destruction from factory closures, travel bans, transport restrictions)
2. OPEC-Russia alliance breakdown (OPEC+ collapse on March 6, 2020)
3. Saudi-Russia price war initiated March 8, 2020

**Duration:** March 2020 – stabilization by June 2020

**Key Players:**
- Saudi Arabia: Initiated price war with $6-8/barrel discounts (March 8)
- Russia: Walked away from OPEC+ agreement (March 6), rejecting production cuts
- Trump administration: Sanction pressure on Rosneft (Feb 2020); later called Saudi Crown Prince, threatening military withdrawal (April 2)
- OPEC: Eventually negotiated 10M bpd production cut (April 9) with Russia

## Price Impact

- **Pre-pandemic:** Normal pricing ~$60+/barrel
- **January 2020:** Already -30% from start of year due to COVID demand forecasts
- **March 8-9, 2020 (price war announcement):**
  - Brent crude: -30% single day (largest drop since Gulf War)
  - WTI: -20% single day
  - Overall quarterly drop: -65%
  
- **March 18, 2020:** 17-year low: Brent $24.72/bbl, WTI $20.48/bbl

- **April 20, 2020:** Historic milestone: **WTI futures went NEGATIVE (-$37/bbl for May delivery contract)**
  - First time in recorded history
  - Caused by: depressed demand + insufficient storage (Cushing, Oklahoma at 81% capacity, 64M of 92M barrels)
  - Canadian heavy oil collapsed below $5/bbl
  - Brent fell to $18/bbl

- **Brent recovery:** Rebounded to $32/bbl by April 3 after Russian-Saudi cooperation signals

## Global Economic Impact

- **2020 stock market crash:** Triggered by combination of oil price war + COVID fears
- **Dow Jones Black Monday (March 9):** -2,000 points (-7.8%), largest point drop in history
- **Circuit breakers:** Triggered multiple times due to -15% intraday moves
- **Global markets:**  S&P 500 -7.6%, NASDAQ -7.2%, Italy's FTSE MIB -11%
- **Producer bankruptcies:** Whiting Petroleum (120,000 bpd) first major producer bankruptcy; Diamond Offshore Drilling filed
- **Shale oil crisis:** Shale requires $40+/bbl to operate profitably; North American drilling cuts followed
- **Global production cuts:** 10% of global oil production uneconomic (Wood Mackenzie estimate) at Brent $25/bbl
  - Hit hard: Venezuela, Mexico, Canadian oil sands (all heavy crude producers)

## OPEC+ Production Response

- **April 9, 2020:** Historic agreement: 10M bpd production cut (first time)
- **Demand destruction:** Expected 6.8M bpd demand fall, later revised to up to 35M bpd
- **USA:** Expected 2M bpd production decline by year-end
- **Mexico:** Proposed 100,000-400,000 bpd cuts
- **Contango market:** Purchasing cheap oil for storage for later sale (unusual market dynamic)
- **Inventory surge:** US crude inventories reached record-high levels; Cushing hit 81% working capacity (64M of 92M barrels)

## Supply Chain & Storage Crisis

- **Tankers:** 104 oil tankers came to US shores to offload oil
- **Storage bottleneck:** Insufficient infrastructure for demand destruction magnitude
- **Negative pricing:** Futures contracts paying operators to take oil off hands due to storage limits
- **Market manipulation trial:** "Essex Boys" (9 independent traders) accused of deliberately pushing prices down via coordinated trading; made ~$660M on April 20; trial ongoing (anticipated to last until 2025)

## Government & Oil Industry Responses

**Saudi Arabia:**
- Saudi Aramco cut capital expenditures: $35-40B planned → $25-30B actual
- Saudi government increased debt ceiling: 30% → 50% of GDP (due to pandemic + oil collapse)
- Saudi budget deficit: expected to rise from 6% → 9%

**Russia:**
- Expected 2020 budget surplus: 930B rubles ($11.4B) → revised to deficit
- Ruble collapsed: -30% between start of 2020 and March 18

**Other producers:**
- Iraq & Kuwait announced price discounts (less aggressive than Saudi)
- UAE announced 4M bpd production (vs 3.5M bpd capacity)
- Qatar minister called it a "very big mistake"
- Norway warned of potential currency intervention (first time in 2 decades)
- Nigeria devalued naira; stock market and bond prices fell

## Key Insights

- **Demand destruction > supply management:** COVID demand collapse was the primary driver, not supply shortage
- **Storage limits real:** For the first time in modern markets, physical storage limits forced negative pricing
- **OPEC+ fragility:** Alliance broke on Russia's walk-out (March 6), showing internal fractures
- **Shale vulnerability:** US shale oil producers revealed fragile at prices below $40-55/bbl
- **Market psychology:** Fear of storage shortage + trading coordination created extreme price distortion (negative pricing)
- **Structural question:** Raised questions about global refinery capacity adequacy and need for strategic reserves
